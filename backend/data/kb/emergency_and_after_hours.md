# Emergency Care and After-Hours Support

## When to call emergency services (108) immediately

Mykare is an OPD clinic, not an emergency hospital. Please call **108** (Indian ambulance service) or go directly to the nearest emergency room for:

- Chest pain or pressure, especially with sweating, shortness of breath, or pain radiating to the arm or jaw
- Sudden weakness on one side of the body, slurred speech, or facial drooping (signs of stroke)
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
- Heavy bleeding that won't stop
- Severe head injury, especially with vomiting or confusion
- Suspected poisoning or overdose
- Severe allergic reaction (swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing)
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm

## After-hours queries

If you have a non-emergency concern outside our operating hours (5 PM – 9 AM, or Sunday), you can:
- Send a message to this assistant — appointment booking and general queries work 24/7.
- For prescription clarifications or follow-up questions, our doctors check messages once in the evening (around 7 PM).

## What this assistant cannot do

This AI assistant **cannot**:
- Diagnose a medical condition
- Prescribe medication
- Provide dosing advice
- Interpret a lab report as a substitute for a doctor
- Replace urgent medical care

If you describe symptoms suggesting a serious problem, the assistant will always recommend you see a doctor or seek emergency care.

## Mental health support

If you are struggling, you don't have to wait. India has several free helplines:
- **iCall** — 9152987821 (Mon–Sat, 8 AM – 10 PM)
- **Vandrevala Foundation** — 1860-2662-345 (24/7)
- **NIMHANS Helpline** — 080-46110007 (24/7)

You can also book a consultation with our General Medicine team, and we can refer you to a mental health professional.
