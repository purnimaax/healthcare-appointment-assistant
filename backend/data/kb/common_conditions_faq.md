# Common Conditions: When to See a Doctor

This guide gives non-diagnostic information about common symptoms and which department typically handles them. It is **not** a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult a doctor for diagnosis and treatment.

## Fever

For most adults, a fever under 102°F (38.9°C) that resolves in 2–3 days is usually viral and self-limiting. See General Medicine if:
- Fever lasts more than 3 days
- Temperature is above 103°F (39.4°C)
- Accompanied by severe headache, rash, breathing difficulty, or confusion

For children, the threshold is lower — see Pediatrics for fever lasting more than 24 hours, or for infants under 3 months with any fever at all.

## Chest pain

Any new, severe, or radiating chest pain is a medical emergency. Call emergency services. For chronic or mild recurring chest discomfort, book with Cardiology. Bring details of: what brings it on, how long it lasts, what relieves it.

## Headache

Most headaches respond to rest, hydration, and over-the-counter pain relief. See General Medicine if headaches are frequent (more than 4 per month), severe, or accompanied by visual changes, nausea, or weakness.

## Joint pain

Acute joint pain after an injury → Orthopedics. Chronic joint pain (lasting more than 6 weeks), morning stiffness, or pain in multiple joints → start with General Medicine, who may refer to a rheumatologist.

## Skin rash

A new rash that is spreading, painful, or accompanied by fever needs prompt attention. Book with Dermatology. Send a clear photo through the assistant if you're unsure whether to come in person.

## Persistent cough

Cough lasting more than 3 weeks should be evaluated by General Medicine or ENT. Bring a list of any blood-thinners or ACE inhibitors you are taking, as these can cause cough as a side effect.

## Hearing loss or ear pain

Sudden hearing loss is urgent — see ENT within 24 hours. Recurrent ear infections in children also warrant an ENT visit.

## High blood pressure

If a home reading is consistently above 140/90, schedule a Cardiology or General Medicine visit. Keep a log of readings over at least one week before the appointment.

## When in doubt

Book with General Medicine. The doctor can examine you and refer to a specialist if needed.
