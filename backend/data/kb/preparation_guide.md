# How to Prepare for Your Appointment

## General preparation

For any consultation, write down:
- Your symptoms — when they started, how often they occur, what triggers or relieves them
- Any medications you are currently taking (name, dose, frequency)
- Your medical history (past surgeries, chronic conditions, allergies)
- Family history of relevant conditions

## Department-specific prep

### Cardiology

- If a stress test or ECG is planned, avoid heavy meals and caffeine for at least 4 hours before the appointment.
- Wear comfortable, two-piece clothing (shirts that can be removed easily for ECG leads).
- Bring any previous ECG, echo, lipid panel, or angiography reports.

### Orthopedics

- If you have recent X-rays, MRIs, or CT scans, bring the films and reports.
- Wear loose clothing that gives easy access to the affected area.
- Make a note of activities that worsen or relieve the pain.

### Dermatology

- Avoid applying makeup, sunscreen, or topical creams to the affected area on the day of the appointment (unless prescribed).
- If you have photos of how a rash or skin condition has changed over time, bring them.

### Pediatrics

- Bring the child's immunisation card.
- For infants, note recent feeding patterns, sleep patterns, and bowel/urine output.
- If there is a fever, note the highest temperature and any medication given.

### ENT

- If you have audiometry reports or previous sinus CT scans, bring them.
- Avoid using cotton buds to clean ears for 24 hours before the visit.

### General Medicine

- For diabetes follow-ups: fasting blood sugar and HbA1c reports from the last 3 months are helpful.
- For hypertension: a log of home blood pressure readings.

## Fasting for blood tests

If your visit may involve a blood test (lipid profile, blood sugar, liver function), an 8–10 hour fast is usually required. Water is allowed. Ask the assistant or call the front desk to confirm before fasting.
