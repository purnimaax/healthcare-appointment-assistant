# Pharmacy and Prescriptions

## In-house pharmacy

The Mykare in-house pharmacy operates during clinic hours (9 AM – 5 PM, Mon–Sat) and stocks all commonly prescribed medications. Generic equivalents are available for most branded drugs at lower cost — ask the pharmacist.

## Filling a prescription

After your consultation, the prescription is shared with the pharmacy automatically and is also emailed to you. You can:
- Collect medicines from our pharmacy immediately
- Take the prescription elsewhere — we don't lock you in
- Request home delivery within a 5 km radius (free for orders above ₹500)

## Refills

For chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, thyroid), refills can be authorised without a full consultation if:
- Your last consultation was within the past 6 months
- The dosage is unchanged

To request a refill, message the assistant with your phone number and current medication list, or call the pharmacy directly. Refills will be reviewed by the prescribing doctor before being issued — usually within 4 hours.

## Controlled substances

Schedule H1 and other controlled drugs cannot be refilled without an in-person consultation, regardless of when the last visit was. This is a legal requirement, not clinic policy.

## Generic vs branded medicines

When the doctor prescribes a brand name, the pharmacist will offer the generic equivalent. The active ingredient, dosage, and quality are identical. Cost savings are typically 40–70%. You are free to choose either.

## Safety: storing medications

- Most tablets should be kept below 25°C in a dry place. Don't store them in the bathroom (humidity).
- Insulin and some biologics need refrigeration (2–8°C). Don't freeze them.
- Always check expiry dates. Expired medication should be returned to the pharmacy for safe disposal — please don't flush them.

## Medication queries

If you're unsure how to take a prescribed medicine, when to take it, or whether to take it with food, ask the assistant or call the pharmacist. We'd rather you ask than guess.
